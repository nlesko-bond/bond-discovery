Overview

What you're building

You will create two pages on your website. Bond configures your Discovery page and provides the code snippets — you do not need access to Bond's admin tools.

Programs page

Visitors browse sessions, classes, camps, leagues, and schedules.

/programs

Register page

Bond checkout opens on your domain when someone clicks Register.

/register

Bond will confirm your exact page paths and provide your slug (for example, coppermine).

Your site footer needs two scripts: the Bond embed script and your Google Tag Manager install snippet. Bond also loads your GTM container inside the programs iframe for register-click events.

Measurement

Analytics setup

Bond handles Discovery events inside the programs iframe. Your GTM footer handles pageviews on your website. Checkout steps inside the register iframe may need separate Bond/GA4 setup — ask your Bond contact.

What Bond configures

Bond enters your GTM container ID in Discovery admin. When someone clicks Register, Bond pushes a click_register event with program and session IDs and names.

EventWhen it fires
click_registerRegister clicked on programs page
click_redeem_passRedeem clicked on Schedule tab (optional tag)
PageviewsYour GTM footer on your site URLs
Checkout funnelBond checkout app — contact Bond support

What you configure in GTM

1
Create a GA4 Configuration tag → your Measurement ID → trigger All Pages.
2
Create a GA4 Event tag → event name click_register → trigger Custom Event click_register.
3
Map Data Layer variables: program_id, program_name, session_id, session_name, product_id.
4
Publish the container. Use GTM Preview on your published programs page and click Register inside the embed.

If you see two pageviews on the programs page, restrict your GA4 Configuration tag to fire only when Page Hostname equals your website domain.

Layout tips

  • Put the embed as high on the page as possible.
  • Sizing is automatic by default — no offset attributes needed for normal (in-flow) headers.
  • If a fixed/sticky nav covers the embed, add data-bond-chrome-offset-px (nav height in px).
  • Use data-bond-chrome-offset-px-mobile when the mobile nav height differs.

Quality check

Testing checklist

Test on the published website — not Webflow preview or WordPress editor preview.

  • Programs page loads Bond content
  • Register opens a new tab on your register URL
  • Checkout loads and footer is not cut off
  • GA4 Realtime shows pageview on your programs URL
  • GTM Preview: click_register with program/session IDs and names
  • Test on mobile
  • Send Bond your live programs and register URLs

Troubleshooting

Common problems

IssueThe Bond page does not load
Confirm the Bond footer script is live and the site was published after adding it. Preview modes often block scripts.
IssueToo much blank space above the embed
Remove extra top padding above the embed. If you set data-bond-chrome-offset-px manually, lower or remove it (sizing is automatic without it).
IssueHeader covers the Bond page
Your nav is fixed/sticky — add or increase data-bond-chrome-offset-px (nav height in px). On mobile only, adjust data-bond-chrome-offset-px-mobile.
IssueCheckout opens on the wrong page
Ask Bond to confirm the register page path matches exactly what you created.
IssueNo events in GA4
Confirm GTM ID was given to Bond, GTM snippet is in your site footer, tags are published, and you test on the published programs page (click Register inside the embed).
IssueDouble scrollbars
Use only one Bond embed per page. Remove fixed-height or scrollable wrappers around it.
